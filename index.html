about

I recently received my Ph.D. in mathematics at MIT, advised by Bjorn Poonen. I will be starting a postdoctoral fellowship at the University of Georgia in Fall 2019. My CV is here.

My main research interest is in arithmetic geometry. My thesis studied how powerful classical Chabauty's method is when combined with restriction of scalars and descent techniques. Kim's nonabelian Chabauty also fascinates me, and I would like to understand how to use restriction of scalars to push that method further. I am also broadly interested in the statistics of Selmer groups, whether in quadratic twist families or families of hyperelliptic/superelliptic curves. I have also worked on sphere packing and fast computation of zeta functions while in graduate school. My papers are on my research page.

I was supported by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship for years 2-4 of graduate school and am supported by the Simons Collaboration Grant #550033 in Fall 2018.

Before MIT, I studied mathematics at Cambridge supported by the Churchill Scholarship. I completed my undergraduate studies in mathematics and computer science at the University of Michigan supported by the Sidney J. and Irene Shipman Scholarship.
